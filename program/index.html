Workshop Program — May 2, 2026

8:30am Registration & Coffee
Check in, pick up your name badge, and grab a coffee before the workshop begins.
9:00am Welcome & Introduction to the Mini Pupper Competition
IEEE R1/R2 Student Activities Committee
An overview of the IEEE R1/R2 Student Conference Mini Pupper Competition, including rules, scoring criteria, and timeline. Learn what your student teams will need to accomplish and how this workshop prepares you to guide them.
9:30am Introduction to ROS2 and the Mini Pupper Platform
Workshop Instructor
A hands-on introduction to ROS2 (Robot Operating System 2) and the Mini Pupper hardware platform. We’ll cover ROS2 concepts including nodes, topics, services, and launch files, all in the context of controlling a quadruped robot. Participants will set up their development environments and establish communication with the Mini Pupper units.
10:30am Coffee & Tea Break
10:45am Inverse Kinematics for Quadruped Robots
Workshop Instructor
A deep dive into inverse kinematics (IK) as it applies to quadruped robots like Mini Pupper. We’ll cover the mathematical foundations: given a desired foot position \((x, y, z)\), compute the joint angles \((\theta_1, \theta_2, \theta_3)\) for each leg. Topics include coordinate frames, the Denavit–Hartenberg convention, geometric approaches to IK, and how these translate to Mini Pupper’s 3-DOF leg design.
11:30am Hands-On Lab: Implementing IK on Mini Pupper
Workshop Instructors & Lab Assistants
Put theory into practice! In this hands-on session, you’ll implement inverse kinematics code for Mini Pupper using ROS2 and Python. You’ll command individual legs to reach target positions and observe the results in real-time on the physical robot.
12:30pm Lunch
1:30pm Gait Planning & Locomotion Control
Workshop Instructor
Learn how to combine inverse kinematics with gait planning to make Mini Pupper walk, trot, and navigate obstacles. We’ll explore different gait patterns (crawl, trot, pace) and how to generate smooth foot trajectories using Bézier curves. This session bridges the gap between single-leg IK and full-body coordinated movement.
2:15pm Hands-On Lab: Gait Programming Challenge
Workshop Instructors & Lab Assistants
Work in teams to program your Mini Pupper to complete a locomotion challenge! Apply the gait planning concepts to make your robot walk through a simple course. This activity mirrors the types of tasks your students will encounter in the competition.
3:00pm Coffee & Tea Break
3:15pm Competition Preparation & Mentoring Strategies
IEEE R1/R2 Student Activities Committee
Practical advice for faculty advisors on how to structure your student team’s preparation for the competition. Topics include team formation, training schedules, common pitfalls, debugging strategies, and how to leverage ROS2 documentation and community resources.
4:00pm Mini Competition Demo & Wrap-Up
All Participants
A fun mini-competition where teams showcase what they’ve built during the workshop. Followed by a Q&A session, resource sharing, and closing remarks. Participants will leave with all code, documentation, and a roadmap to bring back to their student branches.
4:45pm Workshop Concludes
Thank you for attending! Safe travels.
